Where the live complexity is

Lead with the problem, not the AI

AI is a multiplier. It amplifies whatever it touches - a strong process or a weak one. So the discipline is to define the problem first, then see where AI genuinely fits.

Catastrophe claims and the NHC interface

Rising event volume, managed across a public/private boundary.

Rising event volume. Claims are managed on behalf of the Natural Hazards Commission up to the cap, with private cover above it, reconciled behind the scenes. The operational question: how to keep accuracy high while improving speed with fewer manual hands.

Address-level risk pricing is sophisticated, fed by specialist peril data. Updates land annually at renewal. A cautious area: continuous dynamic pricing would cut against customer fairness and the FMA's conduct expectations.

The three capabilities

The real challenge: turning frontline nuance into AI-ready process

AI works as an assistant, not a decision-maker. It performs brilliantly on what is documented well, and loses the plot where human nuance was never captured. Bridging that gap needs three capabilities working together.

1 Understand the operation and its nuance

Sit with the frontline and find the judgement that never reaches the page.

Someone who knows insurance processes well enough to sit with underwriters and claims people, talk through the technical detail of how a process actually works, and spot the judgement and nuance that lives in people's heads but never reaches the documentation.

2 Capture the nuance into the SOP

Structure documentation so it carries the decisions, not just the steps.

Knowing how to structure documentation so it captures not just the steps but the decision points and the judgement behind them. Asking the questions that move frontline people from "this one policy" to systems thinking - how this process and its Level 4/5 documentation affects the whole department's efficiency. The structure and layout of the SOP determines how well AI inherits the nuance.

3 Fit the SOP into the practical build

Know where AI runs, where a human evaluates, and where judgement stays.

Understanding where AI genuinely improves efficiency and where it does not. Where the human stays in the loop because the judgement is the value. Where AI assists, where a human evaluates, where AI can run. Measurement built in: the parameters that show whether AI is multiplying efficiency or amplifying a problem.

The same capability set - operations understanding, nuance capture, and practical AI judgement - applied as one.

People and capability

The people who power it

Redeploy, don't replace

The people freed by AI hold the deepest process knowledge.

As AI takes the manual load as an assistant, the people freed up are the ones who hold the deepest process knowledge. The opportunity is to redeploy capable people into process excellence - identifying which micro-processes need documenting and feeding clean, structured, nuance-rich detail into the SOPs that train the AI.

Resourcing process excellence

Resourcing AI-enabled improvement means resourcing these people.

Resourcing AI-enabled process improvement means resourcing these people. They do not build the AI - they make it work by giving it documentation that carries the human nuance.
?How Tower is currently resourcing this shift is something I'd be keen to understand.
